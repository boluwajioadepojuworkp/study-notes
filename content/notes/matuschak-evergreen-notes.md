# Evergreen Notes — Andy Matuschak

**Source:** [notes.andymatuschak.org](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/)

**What it is:** A note-writing system where notes are "evergreen" — constantly revised, atomic, concept-oriented, and densely linked. Notes ARE the thinking work, not a byproduct.

**How it works:**
- **Atomic:** One note = one concept. Not a page, not a summary.
- **Concept-oriented:** Organized by idea, not by book/course/author.
- **Densely linked:** No folders. Only `[[wikilinks]]` between notes.
- **Evergreen:** Notes are revised over months and years. Never "finished."
- **Titles are APIs:** Complete declarative sentences as titles.

**Why it connects:**
- This is the researcher/thinker version of [[notes/neuper-note-method|Neuper's student system]].
- Both inherit from Niklas Luhmann's Zettelkasten (1960s).
- The "write from memory" principle connects to [[come-studiare/5-step-method|Step 3 of the 5-Step Method]].
- Dense linking builds [[come-studiare/index|semantic memory]] through elaboration.

**Key difference from Neuper:** Matuschak's system is for DEVELOPING new ideas. Neuper's is for LEARNING existing curriculum. We use Neuper's structure now, and can evolve toward Matuschak's over time.
