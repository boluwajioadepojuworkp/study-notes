# The GOAT ME Method — Study Based on How Memory Works

**By u/Salticido** — The most upvoted study advice in Reddit history (8,041 points). Based on a university Memory Psychology seminar. This is the foundation that our entire COME STUDIARE system is built on.

> Memory works in 3 stages: **attention**, **encoding** (storing/associating), and **retrieval** (remembering). To optimize the final stage, optimize the first two.

---

## GOAT ME — The 6 Principles

| Letter | Stands For | What It Means |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **G** | Generate and Test | Quiz yourself. Come up with answers on your own. Even wrong answers help more than reading the right one. |
| **O** | Organize | Outlines, color coding, timelines. Position becomes meaningful. "CIA FBI KGB" vs "CIAFBIKGBCNNUSABBCUK" |
| **A** | Avoid Illusions of Learning | Recognition ≠ Recall. Rereading = fluency illusion. Highlighting = motor activity. Rewriting = hand moves, brain offline. |
| **T** | Take Breaks | Frequent short sessions > one cram session. Sleep stores memories permanently. |
| **M** | Match Learning and Testing Conditions | Study where you'll be tested. OR: study under MANY different conditions for long-term retention. |
| **E** | Elaborate | Think deeply. Make associations. Songs, doodles, stories, visual imagination. Relate to your life. |

## Additional Key Advice

- **Take notes BEFORE class.** Read textbook chapters ahead of time. In class, add to existing notes instead of frantically writing. Attention is the first step of memory.
- **Teach to others.** You can't teach what you don't know. Explaining forces deeper understanding. You'll discover gaps immediately.
- **Preview before reading.** Skim titles, headings, bold words. Build the mental framework first.

## Alternative Acronym: MORBE

Some prefer this ordering: **M**atch conditions → **O**rganize → **R**ecall (combines G+A) → **B**reak → **E**laborate.

## Scientific Validation (from the thread)

**PhD Psychology professor (u/lshdevanarchist):** Confirmed GOAT ME aligns with Dunlosky et al. (2013) ranking of study techniques:

| Effectiveness | Techniques |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Most Effective** | Practice testing, Distributed practice |
| **Moderately Effective** | Elaborative interrogation, Self-explanation, Interleaved practice |
| **Least Effective** | Highlighting/underlining, Rereading, Summarization, Keyword mnemonics, Imagery for text |

> Source: Dunlosky, Rawson, Marsh, Nathan, Willingham (2013). "What Works, What Doesn't." *Scientific American Mind*, 24(4), 47-53.
