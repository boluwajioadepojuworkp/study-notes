# The Bloom Test: Know When You Have Understood Before Making Cards

Everyone tells you to "learn before you memorize." But how do you KNOW when you have learned enough? How do you know when it is time to open Anki and make your cards?

There is a simple answer hiding in plain sight.

## The Insight

Every textbook chapter, every lecture, every syllabus has **learning objectives**. These are not decoration. They are a promise: if you can meet these objectives, you have understood the material at the level the instructor intended.

This is the Bloom Test. Before making any flashcards, check yourself against the chapter objectives. If you can answer them, you are ready. If you cannot, go back and study more.

## How to Do It

**Step 1: Find the objectives.** Open your textbook to the beginning of the chapter. Look for a box that says "Learning Objectives" or "After this chapter you should be able to." If your book does not have them, find another textbook on the same subject. The objectives will be similar.

**Step 2: Turn objectives into questions.** If the objective says "Explain the difference between X and Y," your question is: "What is the difference between X and Y?" Simple. Direct. This trains you to make good questions, because most of us were trained to answer questions, not to write them.

**Step 3: Answer them.** During or after studying, go through each question. If you can answer it clearly, you are ready to make flashcards for that topic. If you cannot, read again. You may have been memorizing words instead of processing ideas.

## Why This Works

Learning objectives were designed by instructional experts using Bloom's Taxonomy, a framework that classifies different levels of understanding. The objectives are not random. They are structured to test whether you truly grasp the material.

By using them as your gatekeeper, you borrow the expert's judgment. You do not need to guess whether you have understood. The objectives tell you.

## What About the Feynman Technique?

Some people say you must explain concepts simply to a child before you have truly understood. I disagree. You can meet the objectives without needing to simplify everything to a five year old level.

Save the simplification for when someone actually asks you to explain. For your own study, just meet the objectives. That is enough.

## The Bottom Line

Pass the Bloom Test. Then make your cards. In that order. Every time.
