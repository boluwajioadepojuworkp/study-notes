# Scientifically Ranked Study Techniques — Dunlosky et al. (2013)

The gold standard meta-analysis of what actually works for studying. Published in *Scientific American Mind*.

---

## Most Effective (High Utility)

| Technique | Description | Our System |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Practice Testing** | Self-testing, flashcards, practice problems, teaching others | [[come-studiare/5-step-method|Step 3: Close + Recall]], Step 4: Write from memory |
| **Distributed Practice** | Studying over multiple short sessions with sleep between | [[come-studiare/recall-schedule|Recall Schedule]] (1h, 3h, sleep, next day, 3 days, 1 week) |

## Moderately Effective (Moderate Utility)

| Technique | Description | Our System |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Elaborative Interrogation** | Asking "why" questions to connect new and old material | "Why chains" in every note |
| **Self-Explanation** | Explaining problems in your own words while learning | Step 2: Read aloud + simplify ("like to a 10-year-old") |
| **Interleaved Practice** | Mixing different problem types in one session | [[come-studiare/index|Implicit Memory — Interleaving]] |

## Least Effective (Low Utility)

| Technique | Why It Fails | Our System |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Highlighting/Underlining** | Motor activity only. Zero cognitive processing. | DO NOT DO |
| **Rereading** | Fluency illusion. Feels easy = not learning. | DO NOT DO |
| **Summarization** | Passive. No retrieval pressure. | Write from memory only |
| **Keyword Mnemonics** | Limited application. Time-consuming. | Use only for very specific cases |
| **Imagery for Text** | Inconsistent benefits. | Use as supplement, not main method |

---

## The Bottom Line

Only **two techniques** have strong scientific evidence: **practice testing** and **distributed practice**. Everything else is supplementary.

Our COME STUDIARE method is built on exactly these two:
- **Practice testing** = Step 3 (Close + Recall) + Step 4 (Write from memory)
- **Distributed practice** = The entire Recall Schedule (1h → 3h → sleep → next day → 3 days → 1 week → 1 month)

Everything else — elaboration, interleaving, mind maps, physical anchors — are amplifiers. They make retrieval practice MORE effective. But retrieval practice + spacing IS the engine.
