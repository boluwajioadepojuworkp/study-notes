# Why Tool First Thinking Destroys Your Study Efficiency

There is a trap that catches almost everyone who starts using Anki or any study tool. I call it **tool first thinking**: the belief that if you optimize the tool enough, you will get better results faster.

## The Symptoms

Tool first thinkers do things like:

- Skip reading textbooks to "make more time for Anki"
- Turn every line of every slide into a flashcard
- Spend hours tweaking settings and add ons instead of studying
- Hunt for the "perfect spaced repetition algorithm"
- Copy paste notes directly into flashcards

These all feel productive. They all feel like work. But they are optimizing the wrong variable.

## The Real Cost

Tool first thinking saves you time in the SHORT term. In week 1, you feel fast. You made 50 flashcards in 10 minutes. You are ahead.

Then week 2 arrives. The new topic requires that you actually understand last week's material. But your flashcards only tested surface level recognition. You press "Again" on every card. You cannot apply what you memorized to new problems.

Now you have to restudy week 1 from scratch AND learn week 2. You lost time, not saved it.

## The Alternative: System First Thinking

People who use Anki effectively think differently. They do not ask "how can I make flashcards faster?" They ask "how can I eliminate waste from my entire study process?"

This means:

- Encode deeply before you make a single card
- Process the material until you can explain it to yourself without notes
- Only then create a flashcard that tests real understanding, not recognition
- Accept that card creation IS studying, not a separate chore

## The Three Parts of Efficiency

Your study system has three parts:

1. **Encoding**: How you take in new information. Are you processing it or just highlighting?
2. **Storage**: How you organize knowledge in memory. Is it connected to what you already know?
3. **Retrieval**: How you practice pulling it out. Are you testing understanding or just recognition?

Tool first thinkers spend 100% of their energy on retrieval (Anki settings, review speed) while neglecting encoding and storage entirely.

A good system balances all three. When encoding is strong, retrieval is easy. When storage is connected, retrieval is automatic. You do not need to obsess over Anki settings because the cards just work.

## The Big Picture

Anki is one third of the puzzle. The other two thirds happen in your brain before you ever open the app. Optimize those first, and Anki becomes effortless.
