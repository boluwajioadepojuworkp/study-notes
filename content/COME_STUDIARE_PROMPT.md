## USER PROFILE: Cognitive & Memory Challenges

This study method is for a person with ALL of the following:

### Neurotransmitter & Energy
- Extremely low dopamine: brain does not naturally tag information as "important, save this"
- Tired/sleepy when trying to study or be productive: mental effort triggers fatigue, not engagement

### Memory Systems (All Affected)
- Short-term memory problems: information disappears within seconds of reading
- Long-term memory problems: information never makes it past the first 24 hours
- Working memory issues: cannot hold more than 1-2 chunks in mind at once; overloads immediately
- Spatial memory issues: cannot visualize or mentally manipulate objects, graphs, or layouts
- Memory consolidation issues: sleep does not efficiently transfer memories from hippocampus to cortex
- Recall issues: even when something IS stored, accessing it on demand is unreliable
- Procedural memory weakness: difficulty translating theory into action
- Episodic memory weakness: difficulty remembering context of when/where something was learned
- Implicit/Pattern recognition issues: cannot see similarities across problems or identify problem types
- Semantic memory weakness: difficulty connecting facts and concepts into a coherent framework
- Prospective memory weakness: forgets to recall information at the right moment (exam time)

### Comprehension & Processing
- Cannot understand easy or hard concepts: both simple and complex ideas feel equally impenetrable
- Cannot understand math, STEM subjects, or programming: technical material feels like a foreign language
- Cannot understand complex or programming languages: syntax, logic, and abstraction do not compute
- Problem-solving issues: cannot break down a problem into steps or see a path to the solution
- Needing to read multiple times to understand/remember: one pass does nothing; even 5 passes may not work
- Cannot remember things even after trying to understand: effort does not translate into retention

### The "Instant Forget" Phenomenon
- Reading and forgetting the next second: eyes move across words, brain retains nothing
- Studying and forgetting the next second: even active study yields zero recall moments later

### Focus & Attention
- Issues focusing and poor concentration: attention shatters within minutes
- Mind wandering: brain drifts away from the page uncontrollably
- Brain fog: a thick mental haze that makes thinking feel impossible

### Emotional & Stress
- Chronic stress: impairs memory formation and retrieval
- Anxiety: constant worry consumes cognitive resources needed for learning
- Social anxiety: fear of judgment in class, labs, or presentations blocks participation
- Difficulty staying calm or focused during exams: exam pressure wipes whatever memory was there

## WHAT THE METHOD MUST COVER

### Exam Types (ALL of them)
- Oral exams (viva, presentations, discussion-based)
- Written exams (essay questions, problem sets, proofs)
- Theory-heavy subjects (lots of reading and concepts)
- Practical/lab exams (hands-on tools, procedures)
- Math and formulas
- Programming and code

### Memory Systems (ALL must be explicitly addressed with specific techniques)
- Working Memory: micro-chunks, externalize on paper, talk aloud
- Short-Term Memory: 3-pass system, immediate vocal recall
- Long-Term Memory: spaced recall schedule (1h, 3h, sleep, next day, 3 days, 1 week)
- Semantic Memory: elaboration, mind maps, "why" chains, compare/contrast
- Episodic Memory: physical anchors, emotional hooks, location rotation, story-ify
- Procedural Memory: hands-on practice, type code from scratch, execute by hand, write procedures from memory
- Implicit Memory (Pattern Recognition): interleaving, compare side-by-side, categorize before solving
- Prospective Memory: trigger-action pairs, exam simulation, implementation intentions (If-Then)

## CORE PRINCIPLES (Non-Negotiable)

1. **RULE ZERO: Never study in silence.** Every piece of information must be read aloud, then recalled aloud without looking. Voice = dual coding (production + auditory). Whisper backup. AI voice passive mode for exhausted days.

2. **STEP ZERO: 30-second preview before every session.** Skim titles, bold words, diagrams. Say aloud what the section covers. Builds the mental shelf.

3. **5-Step Method** for every chunk of material:
   - Step 1: Isolate: take only 2 minutes worth, cover the rest
   - Step 2: Read aloud + simplify: say it like to a 10-year-old
   - Step 3: Close + Recall: stand up, walk, say it without looking. Struggle 10 seconds before peeking.
   - Step 4: Write one sentence: from memory, not copying
   - Step 5: Repeat after 10 minutes: check if it stuck

4. **3-Pass System** for instant-forget phenomenon:
   - Pass 1: Micro: one sentence aloud, close eyes, say it, move on
   - Pass 2: Cluster: after 5 sentences, recall all 5 in order
   - Pass 3: Panoramic: after whole section, recall what each title was about

5. **Enhanced Recall Schedule** (steeper forgetting curve):
   - Immediate → +1 hour → +3 hours → Before sleep → Next day → +3 days → +1 week → +1 month
   - First 4 are NON-NEGOTIABLE

6. **"I Don't Understand Anything" Protocol**: 5-gradient ladder:
   - Gradient 1: Read aloud 3 times
   - Gradient 2: Copy by hand
   - Gradient 3: Find the unknown word, look it up
   - Gradient 4: Replace hard words with easy ones
   - Gradient 5: Draw it (rectangle, arrow, stickman)
   - If still no: post-it "TO UNDERSTAND", move on, sleep, try tomorrow

7. **Terrible Brain Day Protocol** (Green/Yellow/Red):
   - Green: Full 5-step method, new material, recall schedule
   - Yellow: Voice only, no writing, review old sentences only
   - Red: Passive only, AI voice reads, follow with eyes, zero guilt

8. **Dopamine System**: artificial rewards to compensate for low dopamine:
   - After each Step 5: dark chocolate square
   - After 3 cycles: 2 min favorite song, dance
   - End of day: Dopamine Journal: 3 things LEARNED (not studied)

9. **Oral Exam Specific:**
   - 3-Sentence Rule: What it is / How it works / Why it connects
   - Mirror technique: practice looking calm
   - Trigger-action pairs: "When prof says X, I say Y"
   - Pause strategy: silence = thoughtful, not failure
   - What to say if you don't know: "I can reason through it..."
   - Breathing: 4-4-4-4

10. **Practical Lab Specific:**
    - 4-step method: Read once aloud → Write steps from memory → Simulate aloud → Predict results
    - During exam: explain WHY after each command
    - If tool doesn't work: name alternative tool, explain same logic

11. **Body & Environment:**
    - 10 min walk before studying (BDNF boost)
    - 8 hours sleep (memory consolidation)
    - No phone in bed, no screens 30 min before sleep
    - Water every 10 minutes (1.5L bottle on desk)
    - Never study right after eating (wait 30 min)
    - No caffeine or energy drinks
    - Stand or walk while studying
    - Brown noise background (not music, not white noise)
    - 25 min study / 5 min break (Pomodoro)

12. **Emergency Cramming** (30 minutes: damage minimization):
    - Read ONLY section titles aloud
    - One sentence per title
    - Do NOT read full text
    - Close eyes, list titles you remember

13. **Long-Term Memory Forever:**
    - Expanding intervals: 1wk → 1mo → 3mo → 6mo → 1yr
    - Annual "brain dump": blank sheet, write everything you remember
    - Missing items get one 2-min review
