After years of experimentation, I have arrived at a note-taking system that actually compounds. This post explains why most notes fail and what I do instead.

Most people treat notes as storage. They capture information, file it by source, and never look at it again. A year later they have a graveyard of disconnected summaries.

My notes think. They evolve. Every time I open an old note, I revise it. Every time I learn something new, I connect it. Over months and years, my notes compound: each one a brick in a structure that gets stronger, not dustier.

The core principles: one note, one idea. Organized by concept, not by source. Densely linked. Titled with a declarative claim. Written for my future self, not an audience.
